Due:

  • Come to class with the full-scale slopers cleanly and properly drafted and trued on paper.
  • Full scale slopers will be reviewed to ensure precision in drafting.

Before Class:

  • Bring the following materials to class:
    • Half Scale Oaktag Slopers
    • 0.5 or 0.7 mm mechanical pencils
    • Erasers (if needed)
    • Gridded or alphanumeric pattern paper
    • Paper Scissors
    • ½ inch wide MATTE/SATIN FINISH scotch tape
    • Clear straight ruler with 1/8 inch markings
    • Pushpins or sewing pins
    • Fashion French curve/Varyform curve

During Class:

  • Princess panel stylelines
  • Adding fullness by slashing and spreading

After Class:

  • Complete dart rotation, dart distribution, multiple dart transformation, princess stylelines, and added fullness drafting samples and bring them to our next class meeting.
  • Carefully cut and sew the Multiple Dart Transformation Sewing Sample and bring it to our next class meeting.
  • Begin work on the Modular Dress project.
    • Choose one draft exercise or design illustration from pp. 108-120, 136-154 (pp.122-134, 156-178).
    • Modify your Full-Scale front and back bodices ONLY to suit your chosen draft/illustration.
    • Transform your full-scale drafts into patterns by adding seam allowance (SA), hem allowance (HA), notches, tailor's tacks, and requested pattern notation.
    • Carefully cut and sew your final bodice patterns in muslin or fashion fabric.
    • Ensure an opening is left for a closure of some kind.
    • See the Modular Dress assignment brief for details.
  • Read pp.231-233, 239-240, 302-303, 317-318 (pp.275-277, 283-284, 350-351, 365-366) before Class Five.
